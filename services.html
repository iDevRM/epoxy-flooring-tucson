Most Popular

Garage Epoxy Flooring

The most requested service in Tucson. A full-broadcast color flake system bonds directly to your concrete, seals out oil stains, and gives your garage a clean, finished look that holds up under Arizona heat and monsoon humidity.

The problem most Tucson homeowners face: store-bought epoxy kits don’t include a moisture vapor barrier, and Tucson’s summer monsoons push ground moisture through concrete slabs. Without that barrier layer, the coating bubbles and peels within a season or two. Our garage systems include it as standard.

  • Diamond grinding and moisture testing included
  • Full-broadcast flake or solid-color options
  • Polyaspartic topcoat rated for Arizona heat
  • Slip-resistant aggregate available
  • Most two-car garages completed in one day

Typical cost: $1,400–$4,800 for a two-car garage in Tucson.

Premium Finish

Metallic Epoxy Flooring

Metallic epoxy uses pigmented resin that flows and folds during application to create a three-dimensional, swirled effect—no two floors look identical. It’s the go-to choice for showroom garages, home gyms, man caves, and commercial reception areas in Tucson where aesthetics matter as much as durability.

Because metallic epoxy is thicker than standard systems and relies on precise temperature control during pour, Tucson installs require early-morning scheduling in summer to keep the slab below 85°F. Our crew plans for this—you don’t need to manage it.

  • Custom color combinations—dozens of metallic pigments available
  • Unique flow pattern every time—genuinely one-of-a-kind floor
  • UV-stable clear polyaspartic topcoat prevents color yellowing
  • Works on garage slabs, basement floors, and retail spaces
  • Thickness: 80–100 mils—significantly more durable than thin kits

Typical cost: $8–$15 per square foot in Tucson.

Arizona’s Best Choice

Polyaspartic Concrete Coating

Polyaspartic coatings cure faster than epoxy, resist UV fading without yellowing, and stay flexible enough to handle the 100-degree temperature swings between Tucson summers and winter nights. For homeowners who want the best long-term performance from their concrete floor, polyaspartic is the answer.

Standard epoxy can soften and become tacky when garage surfaces exceed 140°F—a temperature Tucson garages regularly hit in July and August. Polyaspartic is rated for continuous exposure above that threshold, which is why it’s our top recommendation for Tucson residential garages.

  • UV-stable—won’t yellow even with direct Tucson sun
  • Cures in 4–6 hours—faster return-to-service than standard epoxy
  • Handles thermal shock from 30°F winter nights to 130°F summer slabs
  • Chemical resistant—oil, antifreeze, brake fluid all wipe clean
  • Can be applied over existing epoxy in some cases

Typical cost: $5–$10 per square foot in Tucson.

What Does Metallic Epoxy Flooring Involve?

The process starts the same as any epoxy installation: diamond grinding, moisture testing, and a penetrating primer. What differs is the base coat application. Metallic pigment is mixed into a high-solids epoxy resin and poured directly onto the primed slab. While the resin is still wet, the installer uses squeegees, blowers, and other tools to move the pigment into the desired swirl and wave patterns.

In Tucson, metallic pours require early morning scheduling in summer because the resin’s pot life—the window between mixing and when it becomes too thick to work—shortens significantly as slab temperatures rise. A slab at 85°F gives you a workable window. A slab at 110°F cuts that window by more than half.

Once the metallic base cures, a UV-stable polyaspartic topcoat seals the pattern and protects the pigments from Arizona’s UV radiation. Without that topcoat, metallic pigments can shift color or dull within a few seasons outdoors or in sun-exposed garages.

Why Do Tucson Metallic Floors Fail Early?

The most common cause of early failure in Tucson metallic epoxy floors is UV degradation. Standard metallic epoxy resins yellow or darken when exposed to Arizona’s intense solar radiation, even inside a garage with windows or a partially open door. The pigment can shift from its original color within two to three years without UV protection.

The second cause is improper surface prep. Metallic epoxy is applied at a higher mil thickness than standard epoxy, which means any contamination, moisture, or surface irregularity under the coating is magnified in the finished surface. Grinding and moisture testing matter more for metallic installs than for any other system.

We address both issues as standard: UV-stable polyaspartic topcoat on every metallic install, and no installation starts until the moisture emission test confirms the slab is within spec. If it’s not, we tell you before we start—not after.

Metallic Epoxy FAQ — Tucson, AZ

How much does metallic epoxy flooring cost in Tucson?

Metallic epoxy flooring in Tucson runs $8 to $15 per square foot installed, depending on floor size, color complexity, and surface condition. A 400-square-foot showroom garage typically costs $3,200 to $6,000. The premium over standard epoxy reflects the higher material cost of metallic pigments and the additional skill required to create a consistent decorative pattern.

How long does metallic epoxy last in Arizona heat?

When installed with a UV-stable polyaspartic topcoat, metallic epoxy in Tucson maintains its color and gloss for 15 or more years under normal use. Without a UV-stable topcoat, the metallic pigments in standard epoxy can shift color or dull within two to three seasons of direct Tucson sunlight. Every metallic system we install includes a UV-rated clear topcoat.

Can metallic epoxy flooring be installed outdoors in Tucson?

Metallic epoxy is formulated for interior use. Tucson outdoor surfaces face direct UV radiation, monsoon moisture, and temperature extremes that exceed the design limits of metallic resin systems. For covered patios or shaded outdoor areas, we evaluate conditions on-site and recommend an appropriate exterior-grade coating system if metallic epoxy is not suitable.

What colors are available for metallic epoxy in Tucson?

We carry dozens of metallic pigment options including copper, bronze, silver, charcoal, blue pearl, champagne gold, and custom blends. Two or more pigments are often combined to create layered effects with depth. We show you physical samples and photos of completed floors before you commit to a color combination.
